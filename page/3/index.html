Weekly market summary - Nov Week 01

by Ziye
2023-11-05
230 words - 2 min read
Job related

Fixed income

BoJ loosened its YCC policy by adding more flexibility to it, allowing the yields to exceed 1%. In the meantime, it announced its 2024 inflation expectation, which signal its preparing to exit the negative interest rate policy.

After NonFarm data was published this Friday, which was below market expectation, yields decreased, serving as a Halloween gift for investors holding long positions in bonds. In the meantime, equity market increased, mainly because the market price-in the expectation that no further rate hikes will increase this year. Also, Bill Ackman’s announcement that he closed the short positions in bond may intrigue a flow of investors to close out the short positions. The market is reacting drastically against the below-expectation data.

Equity market also benefited from the decreasing yields.

Currency

Unexpectedly, Yen weakened after BoJ announced its new decision, further breached 150 after BoJ announced its decision, mainly because the market was putting too high expectation on BoJ’s potential policy move (1.5% or to abandon YCC policy).

Dollar was also weakened, falling to its lowest since September as the Fed meeting and Treasury refunding announcement plunged following the cooler-than-expected US jobs and ISM services data.

Commodity

At the end of the week, WTI Crude oil was seeing sharp losses, mainly due to worries on the US economy, the largest oil consumer, after the release of weak labor market and economy data.

Weekly market summary - Oct Week 02

by Ziye
2023-10-29
240 words - 2 min read
Job related

Fixed income

The gruelling selloff in UST that began since the summer continues, most notably in the longer end of the yield curve. On Monday, the treasury yield breached 5%, a level not seen since 2007 and an increase of about 125bps since the trough in July.

As the selloff continues, markets are putting spotlight on the largest missing buyer: the Fed. The Fed is shrinking its portfolio of government securities at $720 billion annual pace, making the Treasury Departmentâ€™s job of funding a near -$2 trillion federal deficits all the harder. The concerns on the debt ratio kept going. Which, in turn, will affect the future supply of UST.

Equity market was also affected by higher yields in bond market, as it attracts money that might otherwise get invested in equity market. As yields surged in September, the S&P 500 fell about 4.5%, despite relatively positive economic data.

Nevertheless, yields didn’t sustain long above 5% and maintained at a level around 4.8% - 4.9% on Friday close, the market starts to prepare for the coming important meetings and data.

There’s been optimism in corporate credit, as it outperform other assets in the past few months.

Currency

USD/CNY: USD didn’t go for stronger, de-risking is waning. It’s been a week with relatively low volatility, mainly driven by treasury yields and surprises on growth data.

JPY/USD breached 150 on Monday, which may affect BoJ’s opinion on whether to maintain YCC policy.

Weekly market summary - Oct Week 02

by Ziye
2023-10-22
321 words - 2 min read
Job related

Last week’s spotlight was still the violence and the conflict between Israel and Gaza Strip, while there was no significant progress in the conflict so the flows remained volatile.

Commodities

The effects of the conflicts can be clearly seen in the prices of commodities like crude oil, which is about 10% higher now.

Gold also increased by around 2% over the week, and once reached over the point of $2000. For gold, it has the same macroeconomic implications with 10-Year UST, affected by economic growth, inflation rate, and liquidity, also showing the global investors’ preferences for risky assets.

Fixed income

Both derisking flows and selloffs are affecting the treasury market.

The 10-year U.S. Treasury yield hit 16-year highs, but soon recovered from the peak 5%, down to 4.83%. The higher for longer expectation was further confirmed by Powell’s speech last week, which is supposed to be the last public communication before the FOMC on 1st Nov, suggesting further hikes could be needed if sustained economic growth drives more persistent inflation, but no clear messages on if there will be another hike, the consensus are gambling on decreased possibility of a near hike.

There are concerns over the debt ratio, which may not remain the pervious level since the end of COVID due to strong growth in GDP. Now the economy seems to be at its peak (jobless rate is at historically low level) and is expected to be slow, the deficits are still high and are expecting to go higher, a resumption of the upward trend seems inevitable. While it’s not at a crisis level yet, which can be seen from the status of dollar in the global foreign exchange market.

FX

Dollar dropped approaching October’s lows and under the important 30-day moving average support line. Falling Treasury rates last week caused the market to gamble on the decreasing likelihood of a recent Fed hike and for dollar to lose appeal.
